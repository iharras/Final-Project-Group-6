Ethical and Social Implications

The use of facial recognition raises complex issues:

  • 🔒 Invasion of privacy, often without meaningful consent.
  • ⚖️ Racial and gender bias in algorithm accuracy, leading to unfair outcomes.
  • ❄️ A chilling effect on public freedom and behavior.
  • 🔍 Potential for authoritarian misuse and mass surveillance.

Who Should Care About FRT?

Our Target Audience Includes:

  • 🏛️ Policymakers
  • 👨‍💻 Engineers and AI Developers
  • 👮‍♂️ Law Enforcement
  • 🧑‍🤝‍🧑 Citizens and Civil Rights Advocates

Why They Should Care:

  • ➡️ Their choices directly affect how, where, and why Facial Recognition Technology is used.
  • ➡️ These decisions shape the future of our rights, freedoms, and public trust.

What Stakeholders Should Do?

Actions We Recommend:

  • 🏛️ Policymakers: Create clear laws and ensure oversight.
  • 🧑‍💻 Engineers: Build ethical, transparent, and fair systems.
  • 👮 Law Enforcement: Use FRT only with warrants and strong accountability.
  • 🧑‍🤝‍🧑 Citizens: Stay informed and speak up for privacy and fairness.

Each group has a role in making sure FRT is used responsibly and ethically.
